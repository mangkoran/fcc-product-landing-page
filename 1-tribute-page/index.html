Linus Benedict Torvalds

Father of Open Source

Brief timeline of Linus life:

  • 1945 - born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1988 - attended University of Helsinki
  • 1989 - joined the Finnish Navy Nyland Brigade, as part of mandatory military service
  • 1991 - first prototypes of Linux were released
  • 1994 - Linux version 1.0 was released
  • 1996 - graduated with MSc degree. His thesis was titled Linux: A Portable Operating System
  • 1997 - worked at Transmeta in California
  • 2003 - moved to Open Source Development Labs, which then become the Linux Foundation
  • 2005 - created Git, a version control software
  • 2012 - declared one of two winners of 2012 Millenium Technology Prize, along with Shinya Yamanaka

More information about his journey in open source scene could be found in his Wikipedia page
